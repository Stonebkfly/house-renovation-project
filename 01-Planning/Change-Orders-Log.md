# Change Orders Log

## What is a Change Order?
A change order is a written document that modifies the original contract by changing the scope of work, timeline, or cost. All changes to the original plan should be documented as change orders to avoid disputes and ensure clear communication.

## Important Notes
- All change orders must be in writing before work proceeds
- Both owner and contractor must sign each change order
- Update budget tracker immediately when change order is approved
- Update timeline when change order affects schedule
- Keep copies of all signed change orders in 08-Documentation/Contracts
- Photograph before/after conditions for significant changes
- Consider cumulative impact of multiple change orders on overall budget

## Common Reasons for Change Orders

### Owner-Initiated
- Design preference changes
- Upgraded materials or fixtures
- Additional scope items
- Feature additions

### Contractor-Initiated
- Unforeseen conditions (hidden damage, structural issues)
- Code compliance requirements
- Material substitutions
- Method or sequence changes

### External Factors
- Material availability issues
- Weather delays requiring scope adjustments
- Permit requirement changes
- Third-party discoveries (utility locations, etc.)

## Tips for Managing Change Orders
1. Evaluate the necessity vs. desire of each change
2. Consider impact on overall budget and contingency fund
3. Get detailed cost breakdown for each change
4. Understand timeline implications
5. Don't make verbal agreements - always document in writing
6. Review cumulative impact of all changes regularly
7. Keep contingency fund for truly unexpected issues
8. Take time to think through major changes before approving
